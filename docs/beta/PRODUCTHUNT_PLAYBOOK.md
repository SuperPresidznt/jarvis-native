## 1. Pre-Launch Preparation

### Timeline: 2-3 Months Before Launch

**Month 1-2: Community Building**

- Create your ProductHunt profile immediately. Add photo, bio, website links, and social accounts.
- Engage with the PH community authentically - upvote products you genuinely like, leave thoughtful comments, participate in discussions.
- Build relationships with active hunters and makers. Follow people, respond to their content, be genuinely helpful.
- Join relevant communities beyond PH: Indie Hackers, Twitter, LinkedIn groups, Reddit (r/productivity, r/SideProject).
- Share your building journey publicly. Post updates on Twitter, grow your personal brand.
- CRITICAL: Don't just show up on launch day. The community values authenticity and will detect if you're only there to promote yourself.

**6-8 Weeks Before: Create Coming Soon Page**

- Set up a Coming Soon page on ProductHunt (if it's your first launch).
- Share the Coming Soon page across your networks to build your follower count.
- Collect emails from interested supporters who want to be notified when you launch.

**4 Weeks Before: Asset Creation**

- Screenshot design and story flow (4-8 images)
- Product demo video or GIF
- Thumbnail/logo animation (optional but eye-catching)
- Product tagline (one-sentence value prop)
- Product description (detailed but scannable)
- Maker comment draft
- Social media templates (Twitter thread, LinkedIn post, email)
- Landing page specific to PH traffic (with conversion tracking)

**2 Weeks Before: Supporter Outreach**

- Create a spreadsheet of potential supporters:
  - Beta users who love the product
  - People you've helped in communities
  - Fellow makers you've supported
  - Friends/family who understand the product
  - Industry contacts and influencers
- Reach out personally (NOT mass emails) to let them know about your upcoming launch.
- Ask if they'd be interested in checking it out - DON'T ask for upvotes directly.
- Mark who responds positively in your spreadsheet.

**1 Week Before: Final Prep**

- Schedule your launch for exactly 12:01 AM PST on your chosen day.
- Prepare all marketing materials with final links.
- Test your product thoroughly - ensure zero critical bugs.
- Set up analytics tracking for PH traffic.
- Prepare hour-by-hour launch day schedule.
- Brief any team members on their roles (even if it's just you, plan your day).
- Get plenty of sleep the night before - launch day is exhausting.

### Solo Developer Pro Tips

- You can't be everywhere at once. Prioritize:
  1. Responding to PH comments (most important)
  2. Sharing on your own social channels
  3. Personal outreach to supporters
- Prepare canned responses for common questions to save time.
- Use scheduling tools for social media posts throughout the day.
- Block your entire launch day calendar - you'll be glued to your screen.

## 2. Optimal Launch Timing

### Best Day to Launch

**Weekdays (Tuesday-Thursday): Best for Maximum Visibility**

- Highest traffic and engagement from the PH community.
- More competitive - you'll face launches from established companies.
- Best if you have a strong support network and high-quality product.

**Weekends (Saturday-Sunday): Best for Standing Out**

- 15% more "Visit" button clicks than weekdays.
- Less competition from big tech launches.
- Perfect for indie makers, side projects, and solo developers.
- Lower overall traffic but higher conversion rates.
- Easier to reach top 5 with fewer upvotes needed.

**Avoid:**
- Mondays (people catching up from weekend)
- Fridays (people mentally checking out)
- Major holidays
- Days with known major product launches (Apple events, etc.)

### Best Time to Launch

**12:01 AM - 12:10 AM Pacific Time (OPTIMAL)**

- ProductHunt resets daily at midnight PST.
- Maximum time to collect upvotes before rankings finalize.
- Captures global audience across all time zones.
- Shows commitment and preparation (you're up at midnight).

**Alternative Times:**
- 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM PST: Captures mid-day US traffic
- 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM PST: Catches end-of-workday browsers

**Why Timing Matters:**

ProductHunt operates on a 24-hour leaderboard. The earlier you launch, the more time you have to accumulate engagement. A product launched at 12:01 AM has 24 full hours to gather upvotes, while one launched at 6:00 PM only has 6 hours.

### Solo Developer Recommendation

**Launch on a Weekend at 12:01 AM PST.**

Reasoning:
- Less competition means you can reach top 5 with ~150-300 upvotes instead of 500+.
- As a solo dev, you can't compete with VC-backed teams who have entire marketing departments.
- Weekend users are more relaxed, more likely to engage deeply with your product.
- You'll have time to respond to every comment personally.

## 3. Hunter Strategy

### Self-Hunt vs. Finding a Hunter

**Key Statistic:**
- 79% of featured posts were self-hunted by makers.
- 60% of #1 Product of the Day winners were self-hunted.

**ProductHunt's Official Recommendation:**
"Spending lots of time 'finding a hunter' is a distraction. You know your product better than anyone else, and it makes very little difference who hunts the product and how well it performs overall."

### When to Self-Hunt

Self-hunt if:
- You have an existing audience (even small - 200+ Twitter followers).
- You've been active on PH for 2+ months.
- You have beta users or supporters you can mobilize.
- You want full control of your narrative and timing.
- You're comfortable being the face of your product.

**Advantages:**
- You can respond to questions immediately with deep product knowledge.
- No coordination needed - you control the exact launch moment.
- Shows confidence and ownership.
- Community respects makers who launch themselves.

### When to Find a Hunter

Find a hunter if:
- You're completely new to ProductHunt with zero followers.
- You have zero social media presence or audience.
- You're extremely introverted and want someone else to be the spokesperson.
- You have a personal connection to a well-known hunter who genuinely loves your product.

**Advantages:**
- Hunter's followers get notified of the launch (initial boost).
- Experienced hunters know best practices and can guide you.
- Adds social proof if the hunter is respected in your niche.

### How to Find a Hunter (If Needed)

**DO:**
- Look for hunters who have launched products in your category (productivity, privacy, etc.).
- Check their profile - do they actively engage with the community?
- Reach out 2-4 weeks before launch with a personal message:
  - Introduce yourself briefly.
  - Share why you think they'd be interested in your product.
  - Ask if they'd be willing to try the product first.
  - If they like it, ask if they'd consider hunting it.
- Build a genuine relationship - comment on their launches, offer help.

**DON'T:**
- Cold message 50 hunters the week before launch.
- Pay anyone to hunt your product (violates PH rules).
- Use a hunter just for their follower count if they don't understand your product.
- Expect a hunter to do your marketing work for you.

**Where to Find Hunters:**
- ProductHunt's "Top Hunters" leaderboard
- Launchpedia's list of 100+ PH hunters
- Search for hunters who've launched similar products
- Ask in PH discussion forums

### Solo Developer Recommendation

**Self-hunt your product.**

Reasoning:
- You've spent 2+ months engaging with the PH community (per timeline above).
- You have beta users and Twitter followers who can support you.
- You know your product better than anyone - your authentic passion will resonate.
- Finding a hunter takes significant time with minimal ROI (data shows self-hunting works).
- You want to build YOUR personal brand as a maker.

## 4. Building a Supporter Base

### What's Allowed vs. What's Against the Rules

**ProductHunt's Official Rules:**

PROHIBITED (Will Get You Penalized or Delisted):
- Asking for upvotes directly ("Please upvote my product!")
- Mass messaging users asking for upvotes
- Using bots or automation to increase activity
- Incentivizing upvotes (discounts, prizes, payments for upvotes)
- Buying upvotes from third-party services
- Creating fake accounts to upvote your product
- Vote-swapping rings ("I'll upvote yours if you upvote mine")
- Bringing in brand new accounts just to upvote (algorithm detects this)

ALLOWED (Ethical Support Building):
- Sharing your launch link on social media
- Emailing your existing users/subscribers about the launch
- Posting in relevant communities (Indie Hackers, Reddit, Slack groups)
- Asking people to "check out" your launch (not explicitly "upvote")
- Encouraging genuine discussion and feedback
- Building relationships with the PH community before launch

**Key Quote:**
"Please don't. People should upvote things they genuinely like or find interesting, not because they were peer pressured to do so."

### How to Ethically Build Support

**2-3 Months Before Launch:**

1. **Engage Authentically on ProductHunt**
   - Upvote products you genuinely find interesting.
   - Leave thoughtful, helpful comments (not just "congrats!").
   - Participate in discussions and answer questions.
   - Support other indie makers' launches.
   - Build karma and reputation naturally.

2. **Build Your Audience Outside PH**
   - Twitter: Share your building journey, post progress updates, engage with tech community.
   - Indie Hackers: Share milestones, ask for feedback, be helpful to others.
   - Reddit: Participate in r/productivity, r/SideProject (authentic participation, not spam).
   - LinkedIn: Share professional insights about your industry/problem space.
   - Email list: Collect emails from beta users, early access sign-ups.

3. **Create a Beta Community**
   - Invite 20-50 people to test your product early.
   - Ask for genuine feedback and implement it.
   - These users will naturally want to support you on launch day.
   - They're invested in your success because they helped shape the product.

**2-4 Weeks Before Launch:**

1. **Personal Outreach (Not Mass Messaging)**
   - Create a list of people who've shown interest in your product.
   - Send PERSONAL messages (not templates) to:
     - Beta users who actively engaged.
     - People who asked to be notified.
     - Fellow makers you've supported.
     - Friends/family who understand the product.
   - Message template approach:
     ```
     Hey [Name],

     I'm launching [Product] on ProductHunt on [Date]. I'd love for you to check it out and share your honest thoughts in the comments. I remember you mentioned [specific feedback they gave], which really helped shape [feature].

     No pressure at all - just wanted to give you a heads up since you were part of the journey.

     Thanks!
     [Your name]
     ```
   - Mark who responds positively - these are your day-of supporters.

2. **Community Engagement**
   - Post in relevant Slack/Discord communities: "Launching on PH next week, would love your feedback."
   - Share on Twitter: "Excited to launch on ProductHunt on [date]! Been working on this for [time]. Here's what it does..."
   - Don't ask for upvotes - ask for genuine feedback and discussion.

**1 Week Before:**

1. **Create a Coming Soon Page** (if available)
   - Share it to build your follower count on PH.
   - More followers = more people notified when you launch.

2. **Final Reminder to Your List**
   - Email your subscribers: "Launching on PH on [date], would love your thoughts!"
   - Tweet a final reminder.
   - Post in communities one last time.

### Launch Day Mobilization

**How to Ask for Support (Ethically):**

DO SAY:
- "We're live on ProductHunt today! Would love your honest feedback."
- "Just launched on PH - check it out and let me know what you think!"
- "Live on ProductHunt! Any questions about [product]? I'm here all day."
- "Launching on ProductHunt today. If you find it interesting, I'd appreciate you joining the discussion."

DON'T SAY:
- "Please upvote my ProductHunt launch!"
- "Can you upvote my product? I'll upvote yours."
- "Help me get to #1 - go upvote now!"
- "I need 100 upvotes to win - please help!"

**Where to Share:**
- Your email list (personal email, not promotional blast)
- Twitter/X (multiple tweets throughout the day)
- LinkedIn (one well-crafted post)
- Relevant Slack/Discord communities (where you're an active member)
- Indie Hackers (one post with discussion)
- Reddit (ONLY if genuinely relevant and you follow community rules)
- Personal network (DMs to people who've shown interest)

**Timing Your Mobilization:**

- 12:01 AM PST: Launch goes live, share to night owls and international audience.
- 6:00 AM PST: Share to early risers, East Coast US waking up.
- 9:00 AM PST: Send email to your list, post on LinkedIn.
- 12:00 PM PST: Mid-day Twitter push, share in communities.
- 3:00 PM PST: Afternoon reminder, share any traction/feedback you've gotten.
- 6:00 PM PST: Final evening push for late-day browsers.

### Quality Over Quantity

**Important Note:**
Upvotes from new accounts or low-engagement users carry LESS weight and may even trigger spam detection. Focus on:
- Upvotes from established PH community members (high karma).
- Genuine engagement (comments, questions, discussions).
- People who actually visit your product page and spend time there.

One thoughtful comment from an engaged user is worth more than 10 upvotes from fake accounts.

### Solo Developer Reality Check

You likely won't get 500+ upvotes unless you have a massive existing audience. That's okay!

**Realistic Goals for Solo Developers:**
- 100-150 upvotes: Likely to hit front page, possible top 10.
- 150-300 upvotes: Strong chance of top 5, especially on weekends.
- 300+ upvotes: Top 3 territory, Product of the Day possible.

Success isn't just about ranking - it's about:
- Getting genuine feedback from your target audience.
- Acquiring users who actually use your product.
- Building relationships in the community.
- Validating product-market fit.

## 5. Launch Day Tactics

### Hour-by-Hour Schedule (Pacific Time)

**11:00 PM (Night Before):**
- Final check: Is everything ready? Product working? Assets uploaded?
- Get your launch link ready to share.
- Eat dinner, relax, do NOT stress.
- Set multiple alarms for 11:45 PM.

**11:45 PM - 12:01 AM: THE LAUNCH**
- Be at your computer at 11:55 PM.
- Hit "Launch" at exactly 12:01 AM PST.
- Immediately post your maker comment (pre-written).
- Share to Twitter with your first announcement.
- Send DMs to 5-10 closest supporters who are night owls.
- Monitor the page for early comments - respond within minutes.

**12:01 AM - 6:00 AM: International Wave**
- This is when Europe, Asia, and Australia are awake.
- Respond to EVERY comment immediately (set up notifications).
- Share to international communities you're part of.
- Monitor upvote velocity - are you climbing or stagnant?
- Take a 2-3 hour nap if needed (set alarms for comments).

**6:00 AM - 9:00 AM: Early Birds**
- East Coast USA is waking up.
- Post on Twitter again with a different angle: "We're live! Here's what people are saying..."
- Share any positive comments or feedback you've received.
- Continue responding to every single comment.
- Check ranking - are you on the front page? Top 10?

**9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Prime Time Wave 1**
- Send your email blast to subscribers.
- Post on LinkedIn with a professional angle.
- Share in Slack/Discord communities (where allowed).
- Post on Indie Hackers.
- This is peak PH traffic - respond fast to comments.
- Engage with other launches - support fellow makers (builds karma).

**12:00 PM - 3:00 PM: Mid-Day Push**
- Share on Twitter again: "Sitting at #X on ProductHunt! The feedback has been incredible. Still answering questions..."
- Respond to every comment (you should never be more than 15 minutes behind).
- If you're in top 5, share that milestone.
- If you're NOT in top 5, share interesting feedback or use cases.
- Eat lunch at your desk - this is not the time to leave.

**3:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Afternoon Rally**
- West Coast US is fully online now.
- Share one more Twitter update with any wins: "We hit [milestone]! Thanks to everyone who's tried it and shared feedback."
- Respond to comments (if volume is high, prioritize questions over "congrats").
- Check competitors - who else launched today? Where do you stand?
- Final email to close supporters: "We're at #X, the discussion has been amazing - would love your thoughts!"

**6:00 PM - 11:59 PM: Final Push**
- Last Twitter share: "Final hours! We're live on ProductHunt until midnight PST. Join the conversation..."
- Respond to all remaining comments.
- Thank supporters publicly on Twitter/LinkedIn.
- Don't stress about ranking at this point - focus on genuine engagement.
- Screenshot your peak ranking and key metrics for post-launch content.

**12:00 AM (Next Day):**
- Launch day is over. Congrats - you made it!
- Respond to any final comments that came in.
- Do NOT stay up all night obsessing - get sleep.
- Tomorrow you'll start post-launch follow-up.

### Solo Developer Survival Tips

**Energy Management:**
- Launch day is a marathon, not a sprint.
- Coffee is your friend, but don't overdo it (you'll crash).
- Set 30-minute breaks every 4 hours to walk, eat, stretch.
- Have pre-written response templates for common questions.
- Use text expanders for frequently typed phrases.

**Mental Game:**
- You will NOT be #1 Product of the Day (probably). That's okay!
- Compare yourself to other solo indie launches, not VC-backed teams.
- Focus on quality conversations, not just upvote count.
- Every piece of feedback is valuable - you're learning about your users.
- Ranking is ego; user acquisition and validation are what matter.

**Response Strategy:**

Prioritize comments in this order:
1. **Questions about the product** (answer immediately, these drive engagement).
2. **Genuine feedback/suggestions** (thank them, discuss thoughtfully).
3. **Users sharing use cases** (amplify these! Screenshot and share).
4. **Simple "congrats" comments** (heart/upvote, brief thank you).

**Template Responses:**

For "Congrats!":
> "Thanks so much! Let me know if you have any questions about [product]."

For "Looks interesting, will try it":
> "Awesome! Would love to hear your thoughts after you give it a spin. Feel free to DM me with any feedback."

For Questions:
> [Give detailed, helpful answer] + "Great question! [Answer]. Let me know if you have any other questions."

For Feature Requests:
> "This is great feedback! We've heard this from a few others too. Mind if I follow up with you as we explore this? [Optional: Here's why we haven't built it yet / here's our thinking]."

For Critical Feedback:
> "Really appreciate the honest feedback. You're right that [acknowledge their point]. Here's what we're thinking: [explain]. Would love to continue this conversation - can I DM you?"

### Staying on the Front Page

**What Drives Rankings:**
- Early upvote velocity (first 3 hours matter most).
- Sustained engagement throughout the day.
- Quality of upvoters (high-karma users count more).
- Comment volume and discussion depth.
- Time spent on your product page.
- Click-through rate to your website.

**How to Maintain Momentum:**

1. **Keep Commenting:** Every time you respond to a comment, your product gets a micro-boost in the algorithm. Respond to EVERYTHING.

2. **Drive Discussion:** Ask questions in your responses to keep conversations going.
   - "What's your current workflow for [problem]?"
   - "Would you prefer [option A] or [option B]?"
   - "What's the first thing you'd want to try in the app?"

3. **Share Milestones:** When you hit #10, #5, etc., share on social media. This drives new visitors.

4. **Update Your Maker Comment:** Add an edit mid-day with any big wins or common questions:
   - "EDIT 2:00 PM: Wow! Thanks for all the support. Common question: Yes, we have [feature]. Also, for launch day we're offering [discount code]."

5. **Cross-Promote:** Comment on other launches (genuine engagement, not spam). Makers often check out your product when you engage with theirs.

6. **Use Your Network Strategically:** Don't blast everyone at once. Stagger your outreach throughout the day to maintain steady upvote flow.

### If Things Aren't Going Well

**You're Not on Front Page After 3 Hours:**
- Don't panic. Share more aggressively on social media.
- Reach out to supporters you haven't contacted yet.
- Check if you're in a trending topic - sometimes that drives more traffic than front page.
- Focus on quality engagement - a great discussion can still attract users even without top ranking.

**Low Comment Volume:**
- Ask questions in your maker comment to spark discussion.
- Respond to other launches and engage genuinely - karma helps.
- Share interesting aspects of your product on Twitter and ask for thoughts.

**Getting Negative Feedback:**
- Stay calm and professional. Thank them for the feedback.
- Acknowledge legitimate issues. Don't make excuses.
- Share your plan to address it if possible.
- Negative feedback handled well can become positive sentiment.

## 6. Maker Comments

### When to Post Your Maker Comment

**Immediately after launching - within the first 5 minutes.**

A launch without a maker comment feels incomplete and unprofessional. Users will wonder if you're engaged or just dropped the product and left.

### Structure of a High-Performing Maker Comment

**Opening: The Hook (1-2 sentences)**
- Why did you build this?
- What problem were you frustrated with?

Example:
> "Hey ProductHunt! I'm [Name], the maker of [Product]. I built this because I was tired of [specific pain point]. As someone who [your background], I couldn't find a tool that [specific need]."

**Middle: The Solution (3-5 bullet points)**
- What does your product do?
- What are the key features/benefits?
- What makes it different?

Use bullet points for scannability:
> **What [Product] does:**
> - [Key feature 1] - [benefit]
> - [Key feature 2] - [benefit]
> - [Key feature 3] - [benefit]
>
> **Why it's different:**
> - [Privacy-first / No tracking / Your unique angle]
> - [Another differentiator]

**Closing: The Ask (2-3 sentences)**
- What kind of feedback are you looking for?
- Any special offer for PH community?
- Thank the community.

Example:
> I'd love to hear your honest feedback - what features would make this more useful for you? For ProductHunt supporters, use code **PRODUCTHUNT** for 50% off your first month.
>
> Thanks for checking it out!

### Best Practices for Maker Comments

**DO:**
- Write it in advance, paste it immediately after launch.
- Use formatting: bold, bullet points, white space.
- Keep it conversational and human, not corporate.
- Show personality - this is YOUR product, be proud!
- Include a discount code or special offer for PH community.
- Ask specific questions to drive discussion.
- Mention your background/credibility briefly if relevant.
- Add social proof if you have it (beta users, existing users, etc.).

**DON'T:**
- Write a wall of text - people won't read it.
- Be overly salesy or promotional.
- Use jargon or technical terms unless your audience is technical.
- Write it at 12:01 AM when you're tired - prepare it in advance!
- Forget to include a call-to-action.
- Be defensive or make excuses for missing features.

### Tone & Voice

**For a Solo Developer / Indie Maker:**
- Humble but confident.
- Personal, not corporate.
- Honest about limitations.
- Passionate about the problem you're solving.

**Phrases that Work:**
- "I built this because..."
- "I was frustrated with..."
- "As a [your background], I needed..."
- "I'd love your honest feedback..."
- "Still learning, so any suggestions welcome..."
- "Happy to answer any questions..."

**Phrases to Avoid:**
- "We're revolutionizing..."
- "Game-changing AI-powered..."
- "The future of [category]..."
- "Disrupting the [industry]..."
- Anything that sounds like a VC pitch deck.

### Updating Your Maker Comment During the Day

**When to Add Updates:**

Add an "EDIT" to your comment if:
- You hit a major milestone (#5, #3, #1).
- You want to address a common question that keeps coming up.
- You want to share a particularly cool use case someone mentioned.
- You're extending your special offer.

**How to Format Updates:**

### Responding to Comments

**Response Speed:**
- Aim to respond within 15 minutes during launch day.
- Set up notifications on your phone.
- Have ProductHunt open in a pinned tab all day.

**Response Quality:**

For Simple Congrats:
- Heart/upvote the comment.
- Quick "Thanks!" or "Appreciate it!" is fine.
- Don't feel obligated to write a paragraph.

For Questions:
- Answer thoroughly and helpfully.
- Link to documentation if relevant.
- Offer to continue the conversation via DM or email.

For Feature Requests:
- Thank them for the idea.
- Explain if/why it's on your roadmap.
- Ask follow-up questions to understand their need better.

For Criticism:
- Stay professional and grateful.
- Acknowledge valid points.
- Explain your reasoning if there's a philosophical difference.
- Offer to continue the conversation privately if needed.

### Thumbnail / Logo

**Specifications:**
- Size: 460 Ã— 240 pixels (will display as 240Ã—240 square in some contexts)
- Format: GIF, PNG, or JPG
- File size: Under 2-3 MB
- Optimize for a square crop (center of the image)

**Best Practices:**
- **Static Logo:** Simple, clean, recognizable. Works for most products.
- **Animated GIF:** Can make you stand out, but must be subtle and tasteful.
  - Avoid seizure-inducing flashing.
  - Keep animation simple: subtle movement, logo reveal, icon rotation.

**GIF Thumbnail Ideas:**
- Show your product's value: Speed test going from slow to fast.
- Before/after: Messy workspace becoming organized.
- Product in action: Quick 2-second demo loop.

- Purpose-driven: For productivity app, a checkmark completing tasks.

**Tools:**
- Figma: Design static logos.
- Giphy Capture: Simple GIF recording.
- Gifski: High-quality video-to-GIF conversion.
- Screenhance: Turn screenshots into polished visuals.

**Warning:**
"Using a GIF thumbnail will tend to make your product stand out but not always in a good way." If your GIF is cheesy or distracting, it hurts more than it helps. When in doubt, go with a clean static logo.

## 9. ProductHunt Algorithm

### How Ranking Works

**Official Stance:**
ProductHunt keeps the exact algorithm proprietary to prevent gaming. However, based on community knowledge and official hints, here are the key factors:

### Primary Ranking Factors

**1. Upvotes (Weighted)**

Not all upvotes are equal:
- **High-karma users:** Upvotes from established, active PH community members count MORE.
- **New accounts:** Upvotes from brand new accounts count LESS (or may trigger spam detection).
- **Inactive users:** Upvotes from users who haven't engaged in months count less.

**Formula (Approximate):**
Ranking Points = (Upvote Count Ã— User Karma Weight) + Engagement Factors

This is why you can have 200 upvotes and rank higher than a product with 250 upvotes.

**2. Upvote Velocity**

How quickly upvotes accumulate matters:
- **First 3 hours:** Critical for momentum. Products that get 50+ upvotes in the first 3 hours often make front page.
- **Sustained growth:** Steady upvotes throughout the day rank better than early spike that dies off.
- **Late surge:** Hard to recover if you start slow - algorithm favors early momentum.

**Why timing at 12:01 AM matters:** Maximum time to build velocity and avoid dead zones.

**3. Engagement (Comments & Discussion)**

Comment volume and quality matter:
- Products with active discussions rank higher.
- Maker responsiveness boosts ranking (respond fast = boost).
- Quality discussions (back-and-forth, thoughtful questions) count more than "congrats!" spam.

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Total comment count
- Unique commenters (more valuable than one person commenting 10 times)
- Comment depth (replies to comments, threaded discussions)
- Maker response time (faster = better)

**4. Click-Through Rate (CTR)**

From homepage to product page:
- Strong thumbnail + tagline = higher CTR = ranking boost.
- Products with engaging visuals (GIFs) often get higher CTR.
- Users clicking through and spending time on page signals quality.

**5. Time on Page**

How long visitors spend on your product page:
- Visitors who read description, watch video, click through to website = strong signal.
- Bounce rate matters: Quick exits hurt ranking.
- Longer engagement = algorithm assumes quality product.

**6. External Traffic**

Traffic you drive from outside ProductHunt:
- Social media shares, email traffic, community posts.
- Shows you have an audience and marketing ability.
- BUT: Bringing in brand new accounts just to upvote may trigger spam detection.

### Secondary Factors

**7. Hunter Influence (Minor)**

- If you use a hunter with high karma, slight initial boost from their followers being notified.
- Data shows this is MUCH less important than people think (79% of featured products were self-hunted).

**8. Product Quality Signals**

- Complete profile (description, links, screenshots, video)
- Professional presentation (polished assets)
- Maker credibility (complete maker profile, active community member)

**9. Time Decay**

- Rankings reset at 12:01 AM PST daily.
- Products naturally decay throughout the day as new ones launch.
- To maintain position, you need sustained upvote flow.

### Anti-Gaming Measures

**ProductHunt Actively Detects and Penalizes:**

1. **Fake Upvotes:**
   - Bots, purchased upvotes, fake accounts.
   - Consequence: Upvotes removed, product de-ranked or delisted.

2. **Vote Rings:**
   - Groups coordinating to upvote each other's products.
   - PH detects patterns of reciprocal upvoting.
   - Consequence: All involved products penalized.

3. **Mass Messaging for Upvotes:**
   - Sending DMs asking people to upvote.
   - Triggers spam detection if reported.
   - Consequence: Product ranking suppressed.

4. **New Account Brigading:**
   - Bringing in tons of new PH accounts just to upvote your product.
   - Algorithm detects sudden influx of low-karma accounts.
   - Consequence: Those upvotes may count negatively or be removed.

**Red Flags That Trigger Algorithm Penalties:**
- Sudden spike of upvotes from new accounts
- Upvotes coming from same IP addresses
- Patterns of users who ONLY upvote one product (yours)
- Users who signed up minutes before upvoting
- Mass upvotes from users who never engage otherwise

### Hidden Upvotes (First 4 Hours)

**Recent Change:**
ProductHunt hides exact upvote counts for the first 4 hours of launch day. You'll see "XX+" instead of exact numbers.

**Why:**
- Reduces "bandwagon effect" of only upvoting what's already popular.
- Gives smaller products a chance to gain organic traction.
- Focuses early engagement on quality, not just upvote count.

**Impact:**
Early hours matter even MORE now - you can't rely on visible momentum to drive upvotes.

### Practical Implications for Solo Developers

**What You CAN Control:**

1. **Launch timing:** 12:01 AM PST on a weekend.
2. **Product quality:** Polished assets, complete description, working product.
3. **Maker engagement:** Respond to every comment quickly and thoughtfully.
4. **Authentic community:** Build genuine relationships before launch.
5. **Quality upvotes:** Focus on engaged users, not just quantity.

**What You CAN'T Control (Don't Waste Time):**

1. Gaming the algorithm - PH is sophisticated, you'll get caught.
2. Competing with VC-backed launches - you're in a different league.
3. Exact ranking formula - it's proprietary and changes regularly.

**Success Metric Mindset:**

Ranking is a vanity metric. Focus on:
- Quality of feedback received
- Users who actually signed up and engaged
- Validation of product-market fit
- Relationships built in the community

A #10 ranking with 150 engaged users who become advocates is better than #2 ranking with 500 drive-by upvotes who never use the product.

## 11. Common Mistakes

### Pre-Launch Mistakes

**1. Showing Up Only on Launch Day**

> "One of the biggest mistakes you can make is showing up on Product Hunt for the first time on your launch day. It's a dead giveaway."

**Why it's bad:** The community values authenticity. If you only engage when you need something, it's transparent and off-putting.

**Fix:** Engage on PH for 2-3 months before launch. Upvote, comment, participate genuinely.

---

**2. Launching Without an Audience**

> "One of the biggest mistakes startups make is launching without an audience. Product Hunt itself won't generate traffic for you! You need to bring your own supporters."

**Why it's bad:** PH amplifies what you bring, it doesn't create it from nothing. Without supporters, you'll launch to crickets.

**Fix:** Build email list, Twitter following, beta users BEFORE launch. Aim for at least 100-200 engaged people.

---

**3. Skipping the Prep Work**

> "The most common mistakes on Product Hunt are skipping the prep work and ignoring feedback."

**Why it's bad:** A last-minute, underdeveloped launch shows. Users can tell when you rushed.

**Fix:** Follow the timeline in this playbook. Prepare assets weeks in advance.

---

**4. Not Scheduling the Launch**

> "Not scheduling the launch for at least a month (so as to invite others to follow your launch) is an important step that many don't follow."

**Why it's bad:** You miss the opportunity to build followers who get notified when you launch.

**Fix:** If PH still offers Coming Soon pages, use them. Schedule 2-4 weeks ahead.

---

### Launch Day Mistakes

**5. Being in "Sales Mode"**

> "Always being in SALES MODE is a top mistake. Don't make the mistake of selling your product, convincing people to go and upvote your product, it's a turn-off for everyone."

**Why it's bad:** PH is a community, not an ad platform. Hard selling is transparent and unliked.

**Fix:** Be authentic. Ask for feedback, not upvotes. Engage genuinely with questions and comments.

---

**6. Asking for Upvotes Directly**

> "Please don't. People should upvote things they genuinely like or find interesting, not because they were peer pressured to do so."

**Why it's bad:** Violates PH rules. Can get your product penalized or delisted.

**Fix:** Ask people to "check it out" or "share feedback." Let upvotes come naturally.

---

**7. Launching at Bad Times**

> "Avoid the classic blunders like posting at odd hours when everyone's asleep."

> "If you want to get good results from the PH campaign then don't launch the product on Saturday or Sunday." [Note: This is debated - weekends can work well for indies]

**Why it's bad:** Launching at 6 PM PST gives you only 6 hours to build momentum before the day resets.

**Fix:** Launch at 12:01 AM PST for maximum time to accumulate upvotes.

---

**8. Not Responding to Comments**

> "Perhaps a common mistake people make is treating PH as just a launch platform. Don't show up on launch day and never turn back."

**Why it's bad:** PH factors response time into ranking. Ignoring comments kills your momentum and shows disrespect to the community.

**Fix:** Respond to EVERY comment within 15 minutes on launch day. Continue responding for days after.

---

**9. Bringing in New Users Just to Upvote**

> "Make sure you engage people who are already active on Product Hunt. Some teams got quite a bit of penalty for bringing in new users."

**Why it's bad:** Algorithm detects this and penalizes you. New account upvotes count less or may hurt ranking.

**Fix:** Focus on engaging existing PH community members. Quality over quantity.

---

### Product Mistakes

**10. Product Not Ready to Retain Users**

> "The biggest mistake is being ready for the release, but the product isn't prepared to retain users. Not being prepared to onboard and retain users is a big mistake."

**Why it's bad:** You get a traffic spike but users churn immediately. Waste of PH launch opportunity.

**Fix:** Before launching, ensure:
- Onboarding is smooth and clear.
- Core features work flawlessly.
- No critical bugs.
- Product delivers on the promise in your description.

---

**11. Poor Product-Market Fit for PH Audience**

> "Products that don't align with the interests of Product Hunt's community, which primarily consists of tech enthusiasts, developers, and startup founders, may not perform well."

**Why it's bad:** PH community has specific interests. A product for non-tech audiences may get little traction.

**Fix:** Ensure your product appeals to PH demographic: tech enthusiasts, makers, entrepreneurs, productivity-minded people.

---

### Post-Launch Mistakes

**12. Treating PH as One-Time Event**

> "A common mistake startups make is considering Product Hunt as a one-time event. Instead, use your launch as a stepping stone for continued growth."

**Why it's bad:** PH benefits extend far beyond launch day. You miss long-term opportunities.

**Fix:** Stay engaged. Add PH badge to site. Repurpose content. Continue community participation.

---

**13. Not Following Up with Users**

> "Keep the momentum moving forward by following up with those who used your product (either by responding to their comments or via email if you captured it)."

**Why it's bad:** You miss the chance to convert PH visitors into long-term users.

**Fix:** Email everyone who signed up. Ask for feedback. Show you're listening and improving.

---

**14. No Special Offer for PH Community**

> "It's common practice to offer an 'early-access' deal to Product Hunt visitors to thank them for trying out your product and sharing feedback. Not having anything to offer is a missed opportunity."

**Why it's bad:** PH community expects some kind of launch-day benefit. Without it, fewer conversions.

**Fix:** Offer 30-50% discount, lifetime deal, or extended free trial. Make it PH-exclusive.

---

### Team/Strategy Mistakes

**15. Not Having Whole Team Engaged**

> "ProductHunt launch is a teamwork thing. Making sure that all the makers in your team are signed up and engage as much as they can with the community is the key."

**Why it's bad:** Solo responses can't keep up with high comment volume. You burn out.

**Fix:** As solo dev, this doesn't apply directly. But consider: Can you recruit a co-founder, friend, or early user to help respond to comments during launch day?

---

**16. Obsessing Over Rankings Instead of Feedback**

**Why it's bad:** You miss valuable user insights focusing only on your ranking number.

**Fix:** Rankings are ego. User feedback, quality conversations, and actual user acquisition matter more. A #10 ranking with 100 engaged users beats #2 with 500 drive-by upvotes.

---

### Spam/Gaming Mistakes

**17. Buying Upvotes**

> "Buying upvotes doesn't work - upvote sellers may present fabricated evidence of effectiveness, but in most cases, launches are negatively impacted by having votes removed or even being unfeatured."

**Why it's bad:** PH detects purchased upvotes. Your product gets delisted or penalized.

**Fix:** Never, ever buy upvotes. Build genuine support.

---

**18. Vote-Swapping**

> "Vote-swapping seems like an inefficient use of time. Reaching out to people that are actually interested in you or your product seems like a much more efficient way to get support on Product Hunt."

**Why it's bad:** PH algorithm detects reciprocal voting patterns. Both products get penalized.

**Fix:** Support products you genuinely like. Don't coordinate quid-pro-quo upvoting.

## Final Recommendations for Solo Developer

### Your Optimal Strategy

**3 Months Before:**
- Engage on ProductHunt: Upvote, comment, participate (2-3 times per week).
- Build Twitter audience: Share your journey, post progress updates.
- Collect emails: Beta sign-ups, interested users.

**1 Month Before:**
- Create all assets: Screenshots, GIF, video (or skip video).
- Write maker comment draft.
- List 50-100 potential supporters and start personal outreach.

**1 Week Before:**
- Schedule launch for Saturday or Sunday, 12:01 AM PST.
- Final asset review, product bug testing.
- Prepare hour-by-hour launch day schedule.

**Launch Day:**
- Post at 12:01 AM PST.
- Respond to every comment within 15 minutes.
- Share on Twitter 6-8 times throughout day (different angles).
- Email your list mid-morning.
- Personal DMs to supporters throughout day.
- Goal: 150-250 upvotes, top 5-10 ranking.

**Post-Launch:**
- Thank everyone on social media.
- Respond to comments for next 7 days.
- Implement quick-win features from feedback.
- Repurpose content (blog post, case study).

**Realistic Expectations:**
- 150-250 upvotes (weekend launch, solo dev)
- Top 5-10 Product of the Day
- 1,000-3,000 website visits
- 100-300 sign-ups (depending on product)
- Valuable feedback from target users

**Success Defined:**
- Getting genuine feedback that validates or improves your product
- Acquiring 50-100 users who actually use the product
- Building relationships in the indie maker community
- Having a "Product of the Day" badge for your website

Ranking is secondary. Focus on learning about your users and building authentic relationships.

Good luck!
